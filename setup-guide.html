1 Introduction

LeadRipper integrates directly with GoHighLevel (GHL) to automatically sync your scraped leads as contacts and opportunities. When a lead enters your GHL pipeline, your automations kick in immediately - sending emails, SMS, voicemails, and tasks without any manual work.

ℹ️
How it works: LeadRipper creates a Contact in GHL with all the business info, then adds that contact as an Opportunity in your specified pipeline. When the opportunity enters a stage, any automations attached to that stage trigger automatically.

2 Getting Your GHL API Key

You'll need a GHL API key to authorize LeadRipper to create contacts and opportunities in your account.

1

Log into GoHighLevel

Go to app.gohighlevel.com and sign into your account.

2

Navigate to Settings

Click Settings in the left sidebar (gear icon).

3

Go to Business Profile

Under the Settings menu, click Business Profile.

4

Find API Key Section

Scroll down to find the API Key section. Click Generate if you don't have one.

5

Copy Your API Key

Copy the API key and paste it into LeadRipper's settings. Keep this key secure!

⚠️
Security Note: Never share your API key publicly. LeadRipper stores your key securely and only uses it to create contacts and opportunities in your GHL account.

3 Finding Your Location ID

The Location ID identifies which GHL sub-account to sync leads to.

1

Open Your GHL Dashboard

Make sure you're in the correct sub-account (location) you want to sync leads to.

2

Look at the URL

Your Location ID is in the URL after /location/. It looks like: abc123XYZ

3

Alternative: Settings > Business Info

Go to Settings > Business Info. The Location ID may be displayed there or in Company ID field.

Example URL: https://app.gohighlevel.com/location/fl5rL3eZQWBq2GYlDPkl/dashboard

4 Creating Pipelines in GHL

A pipeline represents your sales process. Each stage in the pipeline represents a step in converting a lead into a customer. LeadRipper will add leads to the first stage of your pipeline.

1

Go to Opportunities

In GHL, click Opportunities in the left sidebar.

2

Click Pipelines

At the top, click the Pipelines tab to see your existing pipelines.

3

Create New Pipeline

Click + Add Pipeline. Name it something like "LeadRipper Leads" or by industry (e.g., "Roofing Leads").

💡
Pro Tip: Create separate pipelines for different industries. This allows you to set up industry-specific automations (e.g., different email templates for roofers vs. HVAC companies).

Setting Up a "New Lead" Automation

1

Go to Automations

In GHL, click Automations (or Workflows) in the sidebar.

2

Create New Workflow

Click + Create Workflow. Choose "Start from Scratch".

3

Add Trigger

Click Add New Trigger. Select Pipeline Stage Changed.

4

Configure Trigger

Select your pipeline and the "New Lead" stage. This fires when a lead enters that stage.

5

Add Actions

Add your actions: Send Email, Send SMS, Create Task, etc. Chain them together with delays.

6

Save & Publish

Name your workflow, click Save, then toggle it to Published.

Example Email Template

Subject: Quick question about {{contact.company_name}} Hi {{contact.first_name}}, I came across {{contact.company_name}} and wanted to reach out. We help businesses like yours [your value proposition here]. Would you be open to a quick 10-minute call to see if we might be a good fit? Just reply to this email or give me a call at [your phone]. 💡
Best Practice: Use merge fields like {{contact.company_name}} and {{contact.first_name}} to personalize your outreach. GHL will automatically replace these with the actual lead data.

8 Understanding Slow-Drip Sync

Slow-drip sync sends leads to GHL one at a time, instead of all at once. This is crucial for triggering automations properly.

Why Slow-Drip?

When you bulk-sync 100 leads at once, GHL's automation system can get overwhelmed. Emails might not send, SMS messages might be skipped, and voicemails may fail. Slow-drip ensures each lead triggers your automation workflow properly.

Setting Up Slow-Drip

1

Enable Slow-Drip

In LeadRipper Settings, check Enable Slow-Drip Sync.

2

Set Your Interval

Choose how often to send leads: every 5, 15, 30, or 60 minutes.

3

Sync Your Leads

Click "Sync to GHL" - leads will be queued and sent automatically over time.

ℹ️
Example: With 100 leads and a 15-minute interval, all leads will sync over ~25 hours. Each lead triggers your automation individually, ensuring reliable delivery.

9 Troubleshooting

Leads not appearing in GHL?

  • Double-check your API Key and Location ID are correct
  • Make sure you've run email validation first (only verified emails sync)
  • Check that leads have valid email addresses (not empty)
  • If using slow-drip, check the queue status - leads may still be pending

Automations not triggering?

  • Verify your workflow is Published (not just saved)
  • Check that the trigger is set to the correct pipeline and stage
  • If bulk-syncing, try enabling slow-drip to prevent overwhelm
  • Check GHL's Workflow History for any error messages
